How does Electronic Invoicing works?

The electronic invoicing cycle is happening in below mentioned five simple steps. There are 5 parties involved in the electronic invoicing cycle. The Seller, GSP (GST Suvidha Provider), Invoice Registrar, GST System & buyer.

Step 1: The Seller is the person selling goods. He Creates the invoice and utilizes GSP Provider services to get the Electronic Invoice Number 

Step 2: GSP Provider is the person who will work as an Intermediary for the invoice registration. He receives the Invoice in the electronic format from the seller and sends it to the invoice registrar for Registration. Once the Invoice Registrar confirms the IRN, he GSP provider sends the number to the seller electronically.

Step 3: IR after receiving the invoice from GSP, assigns the electronic invoice number otherwise called as IRN.

Step 4: The GST system receives the IRN and updates its invoice Registry

Step5: The Buyer receives the invoice directly from the IRP and can avail of the tax credit.
